WHY DONATE BLOOD

You are not useless, know your worth by donating blood.

Why you should donate blood and what are its benefits?

Blood donations are crucial for treating patients with medical conditions such as anemia, cancer, blood disorders, trauma, and surgeries where blood loss occurs.
Donated blood is essential in emergency situations such as accidents, natural disasters, and other unforeseen events where immediate transfusions are necessary to save lives.
Patients who receive timely blood transfusions often experience improved health outcomes and recovery rates, especially in cases requiring ongoing treatments.
Blood donation fosters a sense of community support and solidarity, as donors contribute to the well-being of others within their community and beyond.


Adequate blood supply is vital for maintaining public health and ensuring healthcare systems can respond effectively to patient needs.

We need:

  • Nearly 400 new donors a day to meet demand
  • Around 135,000 new donors a year to replace those who can no longer donate
  • 30,000 new donors with priority blood types such as O negative every year
  • More young people to start giving blood so we can make sure we have enough blood in the future
